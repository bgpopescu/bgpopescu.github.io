2 Course Description

Computational social science is an interdisciplinary field that combines methods from computer science and information technology with theories and concepts from the social sciences to analyze and understand social phenomena. It uses computational methods, such as data mining, machine learning, and network analysis, to collect, process, and analyze large datasets from various sources, such as social media, surveys, and government databases. The goal of computational social science is to use these methods to gain a deeper understanding of social behavior and social systems, and to make predictions about future social phenomena. It encompasses a broad range of research areas, such as social network analysis, online communities, collective behavior, and digital inequality.

The course introduces students to applied computational social sciences. The goal of this course is to provide a framework in which students to implement their research ideas and to equip them with a foundational understanding of R and Python. At the end of the course, students will have the essential computational skills and awareness of relevant programming libraries to conduct their research. They will also get the knowledge about where to adapt and expand these skills.

3 Summary of Course Content

The course will convene twice a week. There will be lectures covering elementary programming techniques (e.g. loops, conditional statements, user-defined functions), error debugging, generating clean code, using external libraries, generating reproducible research with R Markdown and Jupyter Notebooks, using statistical analysis, visualizing data, importing data from drives or from the inter- net, scrape data from websites. There will be assignments that have to be completed on a bi-weekly basis. The grades will be 40% bi-weekly assignments, 30% project proposal, 30% final project.

4 Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course the students will be able to:

  • execute basic programming tasks in R and Python (e.g. loops, conditional statements, while statements, etc.)
  • implement statistical analysis
  • generate reproducible results in R Markdown and Jupyter Notebooks
  • visualize information
  • scrape websites
  • parse text documents
  • implement machine learning algorithms

5 Final Project

The final project will have to be an application which will incorporate concepts and approaches taught throughout the semester. The project is supposed to answer an important question in the social sciences and will be expected to be completed individually using RMarkdown and Github Pages. This website will present your analysis and findings. You are expected to present your results using data visualizations, some text explaining the methodology or other statistical findings. Your project need to demonstrate the use of at least the following tools:

  • Data visualizations
  • Data wrangling
  • Functional programming

You will be graded on the following criteria:

  • Coding style
  • Presentation of Graphs
  • Presentation of Tables
  • Creativity
  • Accessibility

You will also be graded on a project proposal, which is supposed to be a summary of the final submission. This is due before week 4 of class. Within this proposal, you should have the following elements:

  • Outline of the research topic
  • Plan about how you will go about collecting the data
  • Methodology about analyzing the data
  • Plan for presenting the results
