Credits: 3
Prerequisites: None
Office Hours: M 1:00-3:00PM

2 Course Description

The is an applied course on statistical methods commonly used in social science research (including political science and sociology) and provides the necessary foundation to conduct your own analysis and to help you interpret the numbers presented in the media. Students will learn how to read statistics in a research context, what data to use for different research topics, to adopt research designs that are relevant for the research question, use statistical tests and draw conclusions based on statistical tests. Students will also learn how to carry out statistical tests using statistical packages, and to interpret results based on their own analyses.

3 Summary of Course Content

The course will convene twice a week. There will be lectures covering statistical concepts applied to political science and practical lab sessions where students will use statistical software to carry out the tests in question.There will be assignments that have to be completed on a weekly basis. The grades will be 30% midterm, 30% final exam, and 40% weekly assignments.

4 Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course the students will be able to:

  • Use statistical core terminology accurately.
  • Organize data using both numerical and graphical methods.
  • Use measures of central tendency and variability to summarize a data set.
  • Carry out tests of hypothesis about population parameters.

5 Assessment

You will be graded on bi-weekly problem sets (40% of your grade) and two exams (each 30% of your grade). For the bi-weekly problem set, you should work within a group. For optimal learning, you will work on the the problems sets on your own and submit that version to me. You will then check the responses with the other team members and submit the updated responses to the problem set. After you tried the answers on your own, you should go back to the team, write down what you think the correct answer is, and submit your assignment. Your final grade for that assignment will be an average of what you submitted originally and the submission after you consulted with your group.
